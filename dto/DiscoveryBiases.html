5 Cognitive Biases To Avoid In Discovery

Leisa pointed out recently in her post that "the Discovery stage is one of the most important – but probably the least understood." She also mentioned that "Discovery is for discovering, not validating". It reminded me of a cognitive bias called confirmation bias. But "what are cognitive biases?" - you might be thinking - let me explain.

My life has completely changed (for the better) after I started learning that we think we make rational decisions every day, but there is a list of behavioral illusions which can screw up our decision making. They're called Cognitive Biases. They are like optical illusions, but instead of interfering with the way we see things, it gets in the way of our behavior when we make decisions and solve problems. Being aware of these biases help us make better decisions.

Here is a list of Cognitive Biases that can affect the Discovery phase:

Cognitive Bias
Confirmation Bias
What is it?

Tendency to focus on information that confirms our assumptions, rather than letting the data in front of us speak for itself.

Negative effect

During research, we might ignore important facts and pain points that users are raising because we want to confirm pre-discovery assumptions

How to avoid?

Do not build preconceptions of what users need before the research. Listen to them with an open mind for anything possible. Don't validate, discover.
